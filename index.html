Informed Consent

Research Participation Agreement

You are being asked to take part in a research study conducted by Sasagi Kageyama and Yuki Kakuda, BYU–Hawaii, Math & Computing Department.

PURPOSE

The purpose of this study is to examine how different visualization styles affect users' ability to analyze the relationship between sleep duration, stress level, and GPA.

STUDY PROCEDURE

It will take you approximately 10–15 minutes to complete this study. You will be shown statistical information visualized using two formats: a parallel coordinates plot and a scatter plot. You will be asked to answer analysis questions and rate your confidence in your answers. The computer program will store your responses.

RISKS

The risks of this study are minimal. You will be using a computer for approximately 10–15 minutes. No personally identifying information will be collected, except for your name below (which is not linked to your responses).

BENEFITS

There are no direct benefits other than the opportunity to see interesting visualization formats. We hope this study contributes to information visualization research.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Your responses will be associated with a computer-generated ID number, not your name. All response data collected will be completely anonymous. Your name is only recorded on this consent form, stored securely, and shredded after the Undergraduate Research Conference.

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may stop at any time without penalty. Your decision will not affect your grades in any course.

Debriefing

In this study, we compared a parallel coordinates plot with a traditional scatter plot to understand which format helps people better analyze relationships between sleep duration, stress level, and GPA among university students.
